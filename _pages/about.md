A Computer Science Ph.D. who was `overfitted` to the academic circle during his doctoral studies is now spending two years on a university podium trying to `regularize` his career.

He attempts to use `data augmentation` of teaching cases to combat the `underfitting` of his research direction, only to discover that his `generalization ability` has long been shattered by the `vanishing gradient` problem of his past.

Now, he can only `iterate` repeatedly within a `local optimum`, praying that the next round of `backpropagation` will bring a slight decrease in the `loss function`.

**Those who have stared into the abyss understand better how to teach others to avoid it.**
